Articles


Overview of Grammar topics:

‘An’, ‘An’, ‘The’ are the only three articles in the English Language.

  • Articles belong to the part of speech called ‘Determiners.’

  • Articles always sit before nouns to determine quantity, reference and specificity.

  • Article ‘the’ sits before superlative adjectives and particularised common nouns.

  • ‘A’ and ‘An’ sit before countable common nouns.

  • ‘A’ and ‘An’ do not sit before uncountable common nouns

  • Countable common nouns beginning with a vowel sound are always preceded by ‘An’.

  • Countable common nouns beginning with a consonant sound are always preceded by ‘A’.

  • ‘The’ is the definite article and is always used to denote anything in particular.

  • ‘The’ can be used before proper nouns with certain exceptions.

  • ‘The’ can be used before countable common nouns and uncountable common nouns in a specific context.

  • Articles have exceptional uses too. Article omitted in various cases in the English language.
